Prapvhu Parashuram
Parshuram is the sixth avatar of Vishnu in Hindu mythology, known for his strength,
righteousness, and role in restoring order on Earth. He is also considered a Chiranjivi (immortal). Parshuram's birth was prophesied to eliminate corrupt
Kshatriya kings who had strayed from Dharma. He is associated with the axe
(parashu) and is believed to have
reclaimed the Konkan coastline by pushing back the Arabian Sea. Key Aspects of Parshuram: Avatar of Vishnu:
Parshuram is one of the ten avatars of Vishnu, representing the preserver god.
Destroyer of Evil:
He is known for his role in eliminating corrupt and tyrannical Kshatriya kings who had fallen from righteousness. Chiranjivi:
Parshuram is considered one of the seven immortals (Chiranjivis) who will appear again at the end of the Kali Yuga.
Axe-Wielding Warrior:
Parshuram is famously depicted wielding the axe (parashu), which is a symbol of his strength and ability to enforce dharma. Reclaiming Konkan:
He is credited with pushing back the Arabian Sea and reclaiming the land of Konkan.
Guru of Kalki:
He is destined to be the guru of Kalki, the final avatar of Vishnu, at the end of the Kali Yuga.
Penance and Teaching:
After fulfilling his purpose, Parshuram is said to have retired to a life
of penance and teaching, often residing in remote hermitages.
Parshuram's Story:Parshuram's story is rooted in the
concept of Dharma and the need to restore balance when evil prevails. He is known for his battles against
corrupt Kshatriya kings,
including Kartavirya Arjuna, and his actions to ensure justice
and order. His birth was foretold to address the growing injustice
on Earth and to bring about a time
